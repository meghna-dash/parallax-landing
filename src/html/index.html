Frequently asked questions.

Have questions? View answers to commonly asked questions or Contact Us.

Why Parallax?

Even with dedicated onboarding flows, products can initially look overly complex and scare customers away. We want to help those users better engage with those products from day one by identifying their sticking points as they navigate and guiding them to the completion of tasks. That way, they can understand the value of the product much quicker and are more likely to continue using the product.

What if I already have an onboarding flow?

Even the most valuable Saas products face a problem with early retention because users are confused by complex platforms and menu options. Even using analytics tools, it is difficult for companies to anticipate everything a user wants to see during the onboarding process. Bloated onboarding flows also turn users away. Our approach to solving this problem is by presenting users relevant guides when they need it. We believe that better initial engagement and guidance can help users better understand and stick with SaaS products (i.e. reduce churn).

How does Parallax compare to other products in the space?

Session replay companies like Hotjar and Fullstory help bridge the gap between users and products, and they collect the data needed to guide users around SaaS products. Intercom tackles a similar angle by helping you directly communicate with customers, but they focus more on leads and initial conversions. There are also lots of chatbot companies like Chatfuel that automatically handle some user concerns, but they aren’t specific to products.

I have a dedicated UX team. Do I still need Parallax?

There are lots of existing products that help companies better understand their users through analytics and session replay, but they all face the same problem: they help the product team see and identify issues, but it still takes time for the designers and engineers to roll out fixes. Even then, many designs are based on how the dev and product teams — not the users — think about the product. Users get frustrated during this extensive process and often end up leaving new products.
